Why It's Important
❮ ❯

Re-wearing clothes reduces waste and pollution.

Every piece purchased second-hand means one less new one produced, which is important because regardless of material, the production of clothing is bad to the environment. Producing synthetic fibers like polyester requires lots of energy, as well as oil that include toxic gases and chemicals.

Many thrift stores directly support charity.

In America, the best-known thrift stores are Goodwill and The Salvation Army, organizations which provide services to the unemployed, homeless, and disabled.

Thrift shopping allows for more creativity.

Provides more varied and interesting materials as inspiration.
